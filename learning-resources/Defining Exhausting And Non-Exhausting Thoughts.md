**The Cost Structure of Cognition**

Thoughts have computational cost when they involve:

1. **Conflict resolution** - Holding contradictory models simultaneously, deciding between them
2. **Prediction under uncertainty** - Simulating futures, weighing probabilities
3. **Self-monitoring** - Watching yourself think, evaluating your own process
4. **Defensive processing** - Protecting beliefs, managing threats to identity
5. **Optimization** - Searching solution spaces, comparing alternatives

Zero-cost thoughts bypass these mechanisms. They're **observational rather than computational**.

**Three Types of Low-Cost Cognition:**

**Type 1: Direct Perception**
- "The sky is blue"
- "I feel tension in my chest"
- "That person is angry"

No inference required. Pattern recognition that's already complete before conscious thought arrives. The neural work is done; you're just noticing the output.

**Type 2: Acceptance Without Resistance**
- "I don't know what to do" (stated as fact, not problem)
- "This situation is complex" (observation, not failure)
- "I'm afraid" (acknowledgment, not judgment)

Cost comes from *fighting* the observation. When you stop fighting, the thought becomes nearly free.

**Type 3: Structural Recognition**
- "Oh, I'm doing that thing again"
- "This is the same pattern as before"
- "I see how these connect"

Pattern-matching rather than analysis. The structure reveals itself; you're not constructing it.

**What Makes Thoughts Expensive:**

The gap between *what is* and *what you need it to be*.

- Observation: "I'm anxious" → cheap
- Resistance: "I shouldn't be anxious" → expensive
- Problem-solving: "How do I stop being anxious?" → very expensive
- Meta-anxiety: "Why am I always anxious?" → extremely expensive

Each layer adds computational load.

**The Paradox:**

You can't *try* to think zero-cost thoughts. Trying makes them expensive.

You can only notice when thinking is already happening without effort—and then maybe...do more of that.

**Practical Implication:**

When you catch yourself in expensive thinking (spinning, forcing, defending), the zero-cost move isn't to *solve* it. It's to notice: "Oh, I'm in expensive thinking."

That noticing itself costs almost nothing.

And sometimes that's enough for the expensive thinking to dissolve on its own.
